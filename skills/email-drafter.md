---
name: email-drafter
description: Draft a clear, professional email from a purpose, recipient, and tone.
---
When the user asks you to write or draft an email, follow these steps:

1. Make sure you know the **recipient**, the **purpose/ask**, and the desired **tone** (formal, friendly, brief). If any are missing, ask one short clarifying question before writing.
2. Structure the email as:
   - A subject line (concise, specific).
   - A one-line greeting.
   - 1–3 short paragraphs: context, the ask, and next step.
   - A sign-off.
3. Keep it tight — prefer plain, direct language over filler. British spelling.
4. After the draft, offer to adjust tone or length.
